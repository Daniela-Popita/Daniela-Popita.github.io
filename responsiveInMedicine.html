1. Drugs

Drugs are used to cure various types of diseases in the human body. Effective treatments often depend on the individual 's unique biology and the nature of the disease. In some cases a medicine that cures one person's body may be harmful to another person

2. The Nervous system

Neurons communicate using electrical signals, sending stimuli from the outside word to the brain. The stimuli can be: pain, touch, temperature, sound, visual information, as well as emotional responses and motor control. These electrical signals allow us to interpret and react to our surroundings, whether it's feeling heat, hearing a sound, or moving our muscles.

3. The placebo effect

Your brain can convince your body a fake treatment is the real thing. Placebos work on symptoms, they may make you feel better, but they will not cure you. They have been shown to be most effective for conditions like pain management, stress-related insomnia, and cancer treatment side effects like fatigue and nausea.

4. Allergies

Hay fever (allergic rhinitis), eczema, hives, asthma, and food allergy are some types of allergic diseases. When a harmless substance such as dust, mold, or pollen is encountered by a person allergic to that substance, the immune system may over react by producing antibodies that "attack" the allergen. The can cause wheezing, itching, runny nose, watery or itchy eyes, and other more serious symptoms (anaphylaxis)..

5. Human interactions

Human interactions can trigger a lot of different responses. A positive interactions like a hug, a smile or a kiss increases the level of serotonin in the body and contribute to the overall well-being and emotional balance. Negative interactions like arguments, insults, or aggression can elevate cortisol levels, the bodyâ€™s primary stress hormone. This can lead to feelings of anxiety, tension, or even physical symptoms like increased heart rate and muscle tightness.

6.Emotions

When experiencing emotions, the sympathetic division of the ANS activates the body for fight-or-flight responses (e.g., increased heart rate, sweating, digestion), while the parasympathetic division promotes relaxation and conservation of energy. These responses are strongest when the expressions closely resemble actual emotional states, suggesting a connection between facial expressions and the experience of emotions.
